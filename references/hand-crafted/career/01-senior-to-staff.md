# From Senior to Staff Engineer

The jump from Senior to Staff is the hardest in engineering.
Senior solves problems well. Staff defines which problems to solve.

## What Changes

Senior:
- Writes excellent code
- Leads technical designs
- Mentors junior engineers
- Delivers projects on time

Staff:
- Defines technical strategy
- Spots problems before they become critical
- Influences without authority
- Raises the bar for everyone
- Thinks in years, not sprints

## The Staff Engineering Mindset

### 1. Impact over Output

Senior measures: lines of code, PRs merged, tickets closed.
Staff measures: business outcomes, developer productivity, system reliability.

A Staff engineer might write zero code in a quarter but create massive
impact through architecture decisions, standards, and mentorship.

### 2. Influence without Authority

As Staff, you dont manage people but you influence everyone.
You lead through:
- Technical vision - where we should go
- Code review - raising the quality bar
- Documentation - making knowledge persistent
- Speaking up - at the right time, in the right forum

### 3. Anticipating Problems

Senior reacts to problems. Staff prevents them.

You see:
- The database is growing fast, itll need sharding in 6 months
- The team is getting bigger, onboarding needs documentation
- The code is getting complex, need better testing patterns
- The architecture is straining, need to refactor before it breaks

### 4. Making Trade-offs Explicit

Every important decision is a trade-off. Staff makes these visible:

"We can build this fast with a monolith (shipping in 2 weeks) or
build it properly with microservices (shipping in 3 months).
Given that we need to validate the market first, I recommend the monolith."

### 5. Raising the Bar

Staff engineers lift everyone around them:
- Writing design documents that become templates
- Creating code review standards
- Building shared libraries and tools
- Documenting patterns and best practices
- Teaching through pairing and mentoring

## How to Get There

1. Expand your scope beyond your team
2. Write things down - design docs, post-mortems, guides
3. Focus on leverage - what has the highest multiplier effect?
4. Build relationships across teams
5. Say no to low-impact work
6. Develop your communication skills
7. Think about the next quarter, not this sprint
