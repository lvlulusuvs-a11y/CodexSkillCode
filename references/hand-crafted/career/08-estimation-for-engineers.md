# Estimation for Engineers

Estimates are not deadlines. Estimates are predictions with uncertainty.

## The Cone of Uncertainty

Phase | Uncertainty
Concept | 4x
Design  | 2x
Implementation | 1.5x
Testing | 1.25x
Deployed | 1.05x

At concept phase, estimate is 4x off.
At implementation, its 1.5x off.
Only at deployment do you know the real number.

## How to Estimate

### 1. Break It Down

Task: Add payment feature
Breakdown:
- Design payment schema: 2 days
- Integrate Stripe SDK: 3 days
- Implement charge endpoint: 2 days
- Implement webhook handler: 2 days
- Add refund flow: 1 day
- Write tests: 3 days
Total: 13 days = 3 weeks

### 2. Add Uncertainty Buffer

Small task (< 1 week): add 50%
Medium task (1-3 weeks): add 100%
Large task (1-3 months): add 200%

3 weeks * 1.5 = 4.5 weeks

### 3. Communicate in Ranges

NOT: "This will take 2 weeks"
BUT: "This will take 2-4 weeks with best guess of 3"

NOT: "It will be done Friday"
BUT: "Im targeting Friday but Monday is more realistic"

## What Affects Estimates

1. Complexity (how hard is it?)
2. Uncertainty (how well do we understand it?)
3. Dependencies (what needs to happen first?)
4. Experience (has the team done this before?)
5. Interruptions (meetings, reviews, incidents)
6. Quality bar (tests, docs, reviews)

## Estimation Anti-Patterns

1. Estimate given under pressure
2. Estimate without understanding the requirement
3. Optimistic estimate (everything goes right)
4. Estimate from authority (manager says 2 weeks)
5. Padding hidden in estimate (no transparency)

## Improving Estimates

1. Track actual vs estimated time
2. Learn from estimation errors
3. Get estimates from the people doing the work
4. Use historical data from similar tasks
5. Estimate in ideal hours, then adjust for reality

My accuracy improved 3x after I started tracking actual vs estimated.
